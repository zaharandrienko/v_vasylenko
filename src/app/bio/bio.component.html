BIOGRAPHY

Vasyl Vasylenko

Vasyl Vasylenko is currently the Music director and Chief conductor of the Kyiv Opera Theatre, Conductor and Music director of the National Presidential Symphony Orchestra of Ukraine, Professor at the Tchaikovsky National Music Academy of Ukraine, and conductor of the Opera studio by Tchaikovsky National Music Academy in Kyiv. Former Music director and Chief conductor of Donbas Opera (Donetsk National Opera Theatre), Artistic Director and Chief Conductor of the International Opera Art Festival “Zolota Korona” (Golden Crown). He is also the former General manager, Artistic director, and conductor of the Odesa Opera House. President and owner of the S. Prokofiev Charitable Foundation in Donetsk and Odesa (Ukraine). Member of the Presidium of the International Union of Musicians in Moscow. Laureate of the Shevchenko National Prize of Ukraine, People’s Artist of Ukraine, and Honorary Ambassador of Ukrainian Culture in Canada.

Maestro Vasylenko was a conductor trainee in the Bolshoi Theatre in Moscow under the supervision of outstanding maestro Fuat Mansurov. During his training, he attended master classes of Evgeny Svetlanov, Alexander Lazarev, Mark Ermler, Algis Juraitis (conducting), Pavel Lisitsian, Ivan Kozlovsky, Mark Reizen (vocal), and Boris Pokrovsky (stage directing).

Being General Director and Artistic Manager of Odessa Opera House Vasyl Vasylenko devoted 10 years of his artistic life to the renaissance of this masterpiece of world architecture and saving the artistic potential of the world-famous theatre. That period was marked by extraordinary creative enthusiasm, bright premieres of operas and ballets, triumphant foreign tours in the USA, Great Britain, Switzerland, and Lebanon, the creation of the International Opera Art Festival “Zolota Korona” (Golden Crown), and it was truly a creative feat of the troupe during the theatre’s total reconstruction.

In 2002 for the first time in history, maestro Vasylenko represented Odessa’s opera troupe in a triumphant tour of 25 cities in the United Kingdom. The opera "La Traviata" in London’s Royal Albert Hall under his conducting received brilliant critical acclaim from the international media, including Daily Post.

Since 2003 Vasyl Vasylenko has been Music Director and Principal Conductor of the Donbas Opera in Donetsk, Sergey Prokofiev’s birthplace. Many music projects (operas: Nabucco, Turandot, Othello, L'elisir d'amore, Eugen Onegin, La Boheme; ballets: Romeo et Juliet, Le Corsaire, Thousand and One Arabian Nights, Spartacus, Sleeping Beauty) in the Donbas region’s capital are successfully implemented thanks to maestro Vasylenko. He was the first Ukrainian conductor who staged "Falstaff" by G. Verdi in Ukraine as well as the Ukrainian opera "Bogdan Khmelnitsky" by K. Dankevych at the Donetsk opera stage. He is also the author of the unique project – the first stage production in Ukraine of the opera "Der Fliegende Holländer", dedicated to the 200th anniversary of R. Wagner with the participation of stage director Mara Kurotschka in cooperation with cult-famous director Philipp Stölzl, production scenographers FETT FILM Company - Momme Hinrichs and Torge Möller from Germany. Thus, due to the efforts of maestro Vasylenko, Donbas Opera became a flagship among opera houses of Ukraine in terms of creative work and repertoire policy.

Vasyl Vasylenko is an Artistic Director of the International Opera Art Festival Zolota Korona/Golden Crown (1998), one of the most prestigious forums of classical music in Ukraine. Over the years Golden Crown has represented around one hundred worldwide opera stars and conductors from all over the world, as well as from La Scala, Vienna Staatsoper, Warsaw Opera, Zagreb Opera, Royal Czech Opera House, the MET, Bolshoi Theatre, Mariinsky Theatres of Russia, National Opera House of Belarus and Moldova National Opera House.

Maestro Vasylenko has participated in many international festivals of opera and ballet art in the USA, Canada, Great Britain, Germany, Switzerland, Spain, Croatia, Romania, Moldova, Belarus, and Russia. In 2004 he headed the International Opera Art Festival Irina Arkhipova Presenting as a principal conductor. He also conducted the opera "Eugeny Onegin" featuring Irina Arkhipova at Chelyabinsk Opera Theatre in Russia.

Vasyl Vasylenko collaborated with such well-known orchestras and opera companies as Mississauga Opera House and New World Philharmonic Orchestra and Choir of Ottawa and Montreal (Canada), Scottsdale Symphony Orchestra of Arizona (the USA), Zagreb National Opera Theatre (Croatia), Constanta and Krajova Opera Houses (Romania), National Bolshoi Opera Theatre of Belarus, National Opera House of Moldova, National Opera Theatre of Sakha Republic, Chelyabinsk State Academic Opera and Ballet Theatre (Russia), National Opera of Ukraine, National Symphony Orchestra of Ukraine and Symphonic Orchestra of National Radio Company of Ukraine in Kyiv, Lviv National Academic Opera and Ballet Theatre (Ukraine), Odesa National Philharmonic Orchestra (Ukraine), etc.

For 10 years at the invitation of the Spanish Concerеlirica company maestro Vasylenko has been representing Donbas Opera with great success at the Spanish music festivals in Toledo, Alicante, Málaga, San-Fernando, Algeciras, Lalinea, Cadiz, Marbella, Granada, León, Torremolinos, Santander in cooperation with opera singers, such as Lucrecia Garcia, Carlos Almaguer, Rosa Lee Tomson, Miki Mori, Andres Veramendi, Kathleen Berger, Martin Iliev, Byung Lee Sam, Christina Obregon, George Meladze and many others.

He also collaborated with such famous opera singers as Luis Quilico, Lyudmila Shemchuk, Paata Burchuladze, Anatoliy Kotcherga, Walter Fink, Shalva Mukeria, Andreas Makko, Stefan Stol, David Jim, Anatoliy Solovyanenko, Vladislav Pyavko, Badri Maisuradze, Emilia Oprya, Mauro Augustini, Maria Bieshu, Victor Tchernomortsev, Ioanna Karvelas, Yury Nechaev, Vitaliy Bilyy, Victoria Lukyanets, Eugeniy Akimov, Alexander Tsymbalyuk, Ludmila Magomedova, Evgeniy Orlov, Manami Hama, Maurizio Graziani, Mati Palm, Dmytro Popov, Maria Knapic, Dan Serbak, Andriy Bondarenko, Andrii Goniukov including ballet stars Vladimir Vasiliev, Nina Ananiashvili, Marian Walter, Ulvi Azizov, Vadim Pisarev, Irina Golub, Eugene Ivanchenko and others.

For some years Vasyl Vasylenko was a member of The National Council on Culture and Spirituality under the President of Ukraine. He is also a member of the Presidium of the International Union of Musicians in Russia (2002), a Professor of S.S.Prokofiev State Music Academy in Donetsk, and a President of the S.Prokofiev Charity Fund.

In 2015 maestro Vasyl Vasylenko joined the National Presidential Orchestra of Ukraine. The orchestra under his leadership conducts successful concert activities related to the presentation of the work of famous Ukrainian artists and the popularization of Ukrainian musical heritage. During this period, the orchestra presented a number of bright creative projects, in particular, the cycle of concerts "Constellation of Opera Masterpieces" with the participation of Ukrainian opera stars Liudmyla Monastyrska, Valentyn Dytiuk, Tetiana Ganina. Andrii Goniukov, Kateryna Strashchenko, Mykola Shulyak, and others, the projects "Music Dialogue: Ukraine-China" (2018), "Ukraine - Austria" (2019), and "Ukraine - Argentina" (2021) within the framework of the modern music festival Kyiv Music Fest, as well "Piano Extravaganza" concerts with the participation of Danish pianist Elizabeth Nelsen and Argentine clarinetist Mariano Rey. In support of Donbas refugee artists, the orchestra held a unique gala concert "Donbas Opera Echoes of Voices" with the participation of leading artists and soloists of the Donetsk Opera Theater. In December (2019 and 2020), on the occasion of the birthday of the deceased Hero of Ukraine, opera singer Vasyl Slipak, with the support of the "Music Battalion" and the Vasyl Slipak Foundation, an Evening of Memory of the outstanding opera singer was held under the baton of maestro Vasylenko. To the 80th anniversary of the Babi Yar tragedy in Kyiv, the orchestra dedicated the performance of the legendary symphonic poem of the Israeli composer Baruch Berliner - "GENESIS - Creation of the World" (produced by N. Slutsker, Israel). On August 24, 2020, the National presidential symphony orchestra under the direction of Maestro Vasylenko presented a solemn gala concert for the Independence Day of Ukraine on Sofiyivska Square in Kyiv with the participation of Ukrainian opera and pop stars in the presence of the President of Ukraine Volodymyr Zelensky. At the same time, in May 2021, the maestro staged a production of S. Gounod's "Romeo and Juliet" with great success on the stage of the Kyiv Opera Theatre and soon led this theater company as the Chief conductor and music director.

On the eve of Defender's Day, maestro Vasylenko held a grand charity concert of the Ukrainian music "HERZ DER UKRAINE" with the participation of the National presidential symphony orchestra of Ukraine, and world-famous Ukrainian artists - Lyudmyla Monastyrska, Dmytro Popov, Andriy Bondarenko, Andrii Goniukov and virtuoso violinist Dmytro Tkachenko at the Berlin Philharmonic. Proceeds from the concert, which was held under the patronage of the Embassy of Ukraine in Germany, at the initiative of the Public organization "Ukrainian Forum" and with the participation of the Ukrainian classical artistic agency UCAM, were aimed at supporting the Armed Forces, as well as maintaining a house for refugees in the Lviv region.

Vasyl Vasylenko is a famous Ukrainian conductor, a founder of a great number of international art projects in the field of classical music art, an initiator of more than 30 opera and ballet productions, a winner of the title “Leader of XXI century”, “Honored Ambassador of Ukrainian culture in Canada”, “People’s Artist of Ukraine”. For his significant contribution to the development of the art of classical music, he was awarded the Order of Merit, Grade III, Commendation Certificate of President of Ukraine, “Best Artist of the year. Face of culture - 2006” Award.
